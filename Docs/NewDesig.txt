====================================================2. CORE PRINCIPLE
Design the platform around the following model:
PLACE → PROBLEM → WORKFLOW → PROVIDER → JOB → HISTORY
Each concept is a primary entity in the system.
PLACE
A place represents a physical property or location.
Examples:HouseApartmentOfficeShopFactoryWarehouse
All activity in the system is linked to a place.
PROBLEM
A problem represents a user need or issue occurring at a place.
Examples:
Fan not workingWater leakageAC installationBathroom renovationElectrical wiring upgrade
Problem reporting is the main entry point for users.
WORKFLOW
A workflow represents the operational process required to resolve a problem.
Examples:
Quick repairInspectionInstallationMaintenanceRenovationConstruction project
Workflows standardize how work is executed.
PROVIDER
A provider is an entity capable of performing work.
Examples:
TechnicianContractorConstruction firmMaterial vendorLabor group
Providers form the supply side of the ecosystem.
JOB
A job represents a specific instance of work executed to solve a problem.
Examples:
Fix ceiling fan at Mahendra HouseInstall AC in officeRepair leaking pipeline
Jobs contain operational details including status, cost, time, materials, and outcomes.
HISTORY
All activities related to a place are recorded permanently.
Examples:
RepairsInstallationsInspectionsMaintenanceRenovationsMaterial purchases
History becomes the digital timeline of the property.

10. MATCHING ENGINE
====================================================

The matching engine connects service requests with suitable providers.

The goal is to optimize for speed, quality, and cost.

Matching Signals

Distance
Travel time from provider to place.

Availability
Provider schedule availability.

Skills
Matching provider skill set with problem category.

Rating
Customer ratings and reputation.

Price competitiveness
Competitive pricing compared to market average.

Inventory availability
Provider already has required parts.

SLA adherence
Historical performance of meeting service deadlines.


Matching Modes

Auto Assign

System automatically selects best provider.

Best used for quick repair jobs.


Marketplace Bidding

Multiple providers submit quotes.

Customer selects provider.


Customer Choice

Customer browses providers and chooses one.


Hybrid Matching

System recommends providers and customer chooses from top options.


Machine Learning Optimization

Over time the platform can improve matching using data.

Optimization targets include:

Higher first-time fix rate
Lower service cost
Faster response time
Higher customer satisfaction


Example Matching Scenario

User reports:

Fan not working.

System identifies:

Category: Electrical repair

Matching engine evaluates:

Nearby electricians
Ratings above 4 stars
Available within next 2 hours

System selects the best match and sends job notification.

Provider accepts job.

Customer receives technician details and ETA.
